Lasagna

Lasagna is a classic Italian dish that's as comforting as it is delicious. It's typically made by layering flat pasta sheets with rich and flavorful sauces and cheeses, then baking it until it's bubbling and golden brown. The pasta layers are often alternated with ingredients like hearty Bolognese sauce (mad>e with ground meat, tomatoes, and sometimes wine), creamy béchamel sauce, and plenty of melted cheese, such as mozzarella, Parmesan, and ricotta. The result is a hearty, satisfying dish that's perfect for feeding a crowd or enjoying as leftovers. Lasagna can be customized in countless ways, with variations including vegetarian, seafood, or even dessert versions featuring sweet ingredients like chocolate and berries.

  1. Preheat oven to 190°C.
  2. Spread a thin layer of Bolognese sauce on the bottom of the prepared baking dish. Place 3 noodles on top.
  3. Repeat the layers.
  4. Sprinkle cheese on top.
  5. Cover the dish with aluminium foil and bake for 25 minutes.
  6. Remove the cover then bake for another 15 minutes.
